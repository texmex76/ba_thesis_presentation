\begin{definition}
A \textbf{prime number} (or a prime) is a natural number which has exactly two distinct natural number divisors: 1 and itself.
\end{definition}

\begin{exampleblock}{Example}
The first five prime numbers are $2$, $3$, $5$, $7$, and $11$.
